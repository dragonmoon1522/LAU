## Introduction

The Universal Accessibility License (LAU) was created to protect the development of open-source accessible technologies, ensuring the right to free access for users, and guaranteeing that creators retain recognition, control over commercial use, and fair compensation when their work is used by third parties for economic gain.

This license applies to this software, **browser extensions, client-side applications, local accessibility tools, and their derivatives**, aiming to prevent any platform, intermediary, or corporation from appropriating tools created for the common good or integrating them into closed or exclusionary models.

It is especially intended for works developed by people with disabilities or teams actively committed to digital accessibility. It may be used to license accessible software, inclusive technologies, extensions, applications, adapted multimedia content (audio, subtitles, TTS, audio description), technical documentation, interfaces, designs, or inclusive digital environments.

This license does not apply to works unrelated to accessibility, even if they were created by people with disabilities.

It is compatible with licenses such as CC-BY, CC-BY-SA, or GPL, provided they do not conflict with the ethical and accessibility principles of this license.
