## Introduction

The Universal Accessibility License (LAU) was created to protect the development of open-source accessible technologies, ensuring the right to free access and preventing their appropriation by exclusionary or closed systems.

This license applies to this software, **browser extensions, client-side applications, local accessibility tools, and their derivatives**, with the goal of ensuring that no platform, intermediary, or corporation can take ownership of tools created for the common good or integrate them into inaccessible or proprietary environments.

It is especially intended for works developed by people with disabilities or teams actively committed to digital accessibility.

It may be used to license:

- Accessible software  
- Inclusive technologies  
- Extensions and applications  
- Adapted multimedia content (audio, subtitles, TTS, audio description)  
- Documentation, interfaces, and inclusive digital environments  

This license does not apply to works unrelated to accessibility.

It is compatible with licenses such as CC-BY, CC-BY-SA, or GPL, provided they do not contradict its accessibility principles.

“Nonprofit use” does not prohibit income.
